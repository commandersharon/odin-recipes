Argentine Milanesa

Description

Argentina's very own fried chicken/meat. Most argentineans make it with a lean tender piece of meat. But my favorite is with chicken.

Best when topped off with a spritz of lemon, or if you're like me some salsa rosada (ketchup and mayo together).

Ingredients

  • 1 pound Veal steaks, or thin sliced chicken breast
  • 2 eggs
  • 3.5 oz of breadcrumbs
  • 1 1/2 cups of canola oil for frying
  • 1 bunch of parsley
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Chop up parsley as finely as possible. Peel garlic and crush
  2. Arrange steaks/chicken breasts on a board and season with half the parsley, crushed garlic,pepper, and salt. Rub them well
  3. If time allows, let the milanesa rest in the the fridge for one hour
  4. Open the eggs and place them in a bowl. Combine with the rest of the parsley and salt.
  5. Beat the egg mixture.
  6. In a separate flat plate, arrange the breadcrumbs on top.
  7. Dip the milanesa into the egg mixture, then place on top of the breadcrumbs in order to coat both sides.
  8. Repeat for all pieces of meat/chicken.
  9. Heat a skillet with vegetable oil at medium-high heat.
  10. Fry the steaks on both sides for about three minutes, until they are golden brown.
  11. After each steak is cooked, remove from skillet, and drain on sheets of paper towels for about five minutes.
  12. Enjoy!
